About

Thomas Sermpinis (a.k.a. Cr0wTom) is a distinguished security architect and researcher currently serving as Device Security Engineer for TFH. In this capacity, he architects and engineers innovative solutions that shape the future of security for next-generation hardware ecosystems. Concurrently, he guides the strategic direction of Auxilium Pentest Labs as a member of the Supervisory Board.

Previously, as CTO of Auxilium, Thomas directed over 300 security assessments for top-tier automotive OEMs, ensuring the safety of global vehicle fleets. Under his leadership, his team achieved industry-leading research results, including a 1st place victory at the DEF CON Car Hacking Village CTF 2025. A recognized expert in embedded and low-level security, Thomas holds OSCP and OSCE certifications and has a proven track record of finding critical zero-day vulnerabilities in products from Google, Qualcomm, and Nissan.

Beyond his corporate roles, Thomas is an active angel investor, advisor, and the founder of Cr0w’s Place. He is a frequent speaker at top-tier conferences (DEF CON, Zer0Con, TROOPERS) and remains deeply involved in the community through educational content creation and open-source contributions.
